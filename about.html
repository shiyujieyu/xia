star

Stars refer to celestial bodies in the universe visible to the naked eye. The activity of the energy inside the star makes the shape of the star irregular. Stars can be roughly divided into planets, stars, comets, white dwarfs, etc.

star

Stars are giant spheres made of luminous plasma, mainly hydrogen, helium, and traces of heavier elements. On a fine night, there are always countless spots of light embedded in the night. Except for a few planets, most of them are stars.

Planet

Planet, usually refers to the celestial body that does not emit light by itself and orbits the star. Its revolution direction is often the same as the rotation direction of the star around it. Generally speaking, a planet needs to have a certain mass. The mass of the planet should be large enough and approximately spherical, and it cannot undergo nuclear fusion reaction like a star.

Comet

Comet refers to a celestial body moving around the sun that enters the solar system whose brightness and shape will change with the change of the solar distance. It has a unique appearance like a cloud. Comets are divided into three parts: nucleus, coma, and tail. The comet nucleus is composed of ice material. When the comet approaches the star, the comet material sublimates, forming a hazy coma and a tail composed of a thin stream of material around the ice nucleus. The mass and density of a comet is very small. When it is far away from the sun, it is just a "dirty snowball" that is frozen by water, ammonia, methane, etc., and mixed with many solid dust particles. When approaching the sun, the comet decomposes into a comet head and a comet tail under the action of solar radiation, like a broomstick.
