Terrestrial planets are planets with silicate stone as the main component. They are very different from Jupiter-like planets, because those gas planets are mainly composed of hydrogen, helium and water, etc., and do not necessarily have solid surfaces. The structure of terrestrial planets is roughly the same: a metallic center is mainly iron, and the outer layer is surrounded by a silicate mantle. They generally have canyons, craters, mountains and volcanoes on their surfaces. The atmospheres of terrestrial planets are regenerative atmospheres, which are different from the native atmosphere of the solar nebula directly from the Jupiter planets. According to data publicly provided by NASA's Exoplanet Archive, there are 4,043 exoplanets, of which 2,345 are confirmed by Kepler. Kepler has detected about 50 near-Earth-sized habitable zone candidate planets, of which more than 30 have been confirmed. There are 389 confirmed planets discovered by K2.

Kepler-452b

Kepler-452b (KOI-7016.01) is an extrasolar planet orbiting the G-type main sequence star Kepler-452 in Cygnus, about 1402 light years away from the Earth. The Kepler Space Telescope observed it, and NASA announced its discovery on July 23, 2015. Kepler-452b is the first potential super-Earth rocky planet discovered, orbiting in the habitable zone of a sun-like star. According to its physical characteristics, it is sometimes referred to by the media as Earth 2.0 or Earth’s cousin. The mass of Kepler-452b may be five times that of the Earth, and its surface gravity is almost twice that of the Earth, although the mass calculations of exoplanets are only rough estimates. If it is a terrestrial planet, it may be a super-Earth with many active volcanoes due to its high mass and density. From the perspective of space, most of its surface will be covered by dense and foggy clouds. However, its mass is 5 times the mass of the Earth, which also increases the possibility that the planet is a mini-Neptune instead of a super-Earth.

Kepler-186f

Kepler-186f is an extrasolar planet orbiting the red dwarf star Kepler 186, about 492 light-years from the Earth. This planet is the first exoplanet found near a star other than the sun with an inner radius of the habitable zone similar to that of the Earth. NASA’s Kepler Space Telescope detected Kepler-186f and four other exoplanets that are closer to the parent star and have a radius slightly larger than that of the Earth. Kepler-186f is not only similar in size to the Earth, but the distance from the surrounding stars is also just right, making it possible for liquid water to exist on the surface. The various characteristics of this planet make it possible to have characteristics similar to those of the earth. Kepler-186f is the outermost planet in its star galaxy. It is on the edge of the habitable zone, which means that its surface temperature may be too low and the water may freeze. The location of this planet in a habitable area does not mean that it is really habitable. This planet is a cousin of the earth, but it is not a twin.

Gliese 581c

Gliese 581c is the first terrestrial planet discovered in the world that may be suitable for human habitation. It is located in the constellation Libra not far from the earth. Its mass is 5.5 times that of the earth and its size is 1.5 times that of the earth. It revolves around the red dwarf star numbered "Gliese581" about 20.5 light-years away from the earth, and its distance from the red dwarf star is approximately one-fourth of the distance from the earth to the sun. According to reports, Gliese 581c is similar in size to the earth, may have liquid water, and is about 120 trillion miles away from the earth. The orbiting star of Gliese 581c is a "red dwarf", which is smaller, darker, and colder than the sun. Astronomers are still not sure whether 581c is a rocky planet like the Earth, or a very low temperature ice ball with liquid water flowing on the surface. Astronomers say they may eventually find dozens or even hundreds of habitable planets. But in any case, 581c will definitely be included in the annals of astronomy as the first habitable planet outside the solar system.

The discussion about whether there are terrestrial planets outside the solar system, and even whether there is life on these planets, has continued. With the continuous advancement of space exploration technology, astronomers have discovered more and more terrestrial planets, and the research on this aspect has also been paid attention to by scientists. A few days ago, astronomers from NASA have found new evidence through the analysis of spectra in a study to confirm that terrestrial planets do exist and that life is likely to exist on these planets. In the next ten years, when astronomers can study terrestrial planets outside the solar system, they may find that the plants growing on those planets are not green, but may be yellow, orange, or red. The color of the plant depends on the color of the stars in the galaxy where the planet is located and the composition of the atmosphere surrounding the planet.
